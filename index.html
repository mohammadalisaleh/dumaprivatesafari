Tanzania's Most Extraordinary Places

We know these destinations not as points on a map, but as living, breathing worlds — each one different, each one irreplaceable.

Northern Tanzania

The classic safari heartland

Northern Circuit

The Serengeti

The Serengeti is not merely Tanzania's most celebrated park — it is one of the world's last truly wild places. The annual migration of more than a million wildebeest across its endless plains is the greatest wildlife spectacle on earth, a drama that has played out for millennia and shows no sign of wearying. Yet the Serengeti is far more than its migration. It is a place of extraordinary predator density, of lions that live in extended families of twenty or more, of cheetahs that sprint across the kopjes at dawn, of leopards that drape themselves over fever trees in the long afternoon light.

Plan a Serengeti Safari
Northern Circuit

Ngorongoro Crater

The Ngorongoro Crater is the world's largest intact volcanic caldera — a vast, collapsed bowl of ancient geology that cradles one of Africa's most remarkable concentrations of wildlife. Some 25,000 large animals inhabit its 260 square kilometres, including the rare black rhinoceros, prides of lion, vast herds of wildebeest, zebra, and buffalo, and the elegant crowned cranes that wade through the soda lake at its heart. Descending into the crater at dawn, the mist still filling the valley floor, is one of those moments that recalibrates the meaning of awe.

Plan a Ngorongoro Safari
Northern Circuit

Tarangire National Park

Tarangire is the Africa of ancient baobabs and thronging elephant herds — a park that has been somewhat overshadowed by its more famous northern neighbours, and is all the better for it. During the dry season, the Tarangire River becomes a lifeline for some of Africa's largest elephant concentrations, with herds of 200 or more gathering at the water's edge while the baobabs stand sentinel overhead, some of them older than human memory. Tarangire also harbours the rare fringe-eared oryx and the stately gerenuk, and its bird life is, quite simply, exceptional.

Plan a Tarangire Safari
Southern Tanzania

Tanzania's wild, untouched south

Southern Circuit

Ruaha National Park

Tanzania's largest national park, and one of its least visited — which is exactly the point. Ruaha's vast baobab-studded plains harbour Africa's largest population of wild dogs, extraordinary lion prides, and a wilderness that feels genuinely remote. For the traveller who has seen the north and seeks something deeper, Ruaha is the answer.

Plan Your Visit →
Southern Circuit

Nyerere / Selous

Africa's largest protected area. The Rufiji River system weaves through a labyrinth of channels, lakes and forest — a landscape uniquely suited to boat safaris and walking with experienced trackers. Nyerere offers a different rhythm: slower, more intimate, profoundly immersive.

The Islands

Where the safari meets the sea

The Spice Island

Zanzibar

Stone Town's labyrinthine streets. Spice tours through plantations heavy with clove and cardamom. The white arc of the north coast at Nungwi. The turquoise shallows of the east coast at low tide. Zanzibar is the perfect final act to any Tanzania safari — a place to exhale, to swim, to eat extraordinarily well by the Indian Ocean.

The Hidden Island

Pemba Island

If Zanzibar is Tanzania's celebrated island, Pemba is its secret. Covered in clove plantations, fringed by diving reefs of world-class calibre, and almost entirely untroubled by mass tourism — Pemba is where discerning travellers come when they truly wish to disappear.

Our Story

Tanzania is not what we do.
It is who we are.

Duma Private Safaris was founded by someone who knows Tanzania not as a destination, but as home. Born and raised on this remarkable soil — in the shadow of Kilimanjaro, with the Serengeti in the school atlas — our founder grew up understanding this country in the way only those raised within it can.

An education abroad brought new perspective. Exposure to how the world's great travel companies operated — their standards, their language, their obsession with the guest experience — brought clarity. And a return to Tanzania brought purpose.
